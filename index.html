What makes swimming so difficult?

Intro

Why is swimming so difficult? It looks easy on the TV, but whenever you try to do it, you don't go nearly as fast. I'm Stone, and I chose this as my question for Genius Hour.

My Motivation

I wanted to figure out how a sport I partake in a lot is challenging, both so I can relate, and so I can see it from other people's perspectives. I want to know the physical and mental challenges of swimming, and why people shy away from the competitiveness and sport of it. I have been swimming competitively since the age of 9 and have been learning since I was 11 months old. The rest of my family are not as focused on swimming, and some of them have been trying to learn recently. I want to see the difficulties they are going through. as well as the ones I went through when I initially started. This project will teach me and others about the difficulties, both physically and mentally, in this sport.

Findings

I used paper notes with bullet points to take my notes. It helped keep my notes concise and focus on only the important things. It turns out swimming is difficult for a multitude of reasons. Mental reasons include anxiety aned fear of failure pre-race, spoken about in the Competitor® article, and highs and lows from swim meets, which can cause mental instability, spoken about in the Olympic article. Some physical difficulties are a transition from air to water, swimming being a full body sport, and the technique needed, a result of there being four strokes. This is discussed in the Traindaly article.

Mental Difficulties

Fear of failure and anxiety pre-race provide an extra hurdle towards dropping time. they are a distraction and swimmers must push through it. Highs and lows, as seen in the Olympic article, can damage a swimmers' mental health. Michael Phelps got post-Olympic depression as a result, which is on the more extreme end.

Physical Difficulties

Another difficulty I found is the different enviroment, explained in the Traindaly article. Because swimming is in water, which is 800 times more dense than air, there is a lot more physical resistance than any other sport. Your body is not used to it, so it is hard to move. Being in water also forces controlled breathing, which is not required in most sports. It makes the sport a lot more difficult, as the Traindaly article details. Unlike many other sports, swimming is full body. Swimmers need to be in peak condition in all areas to get a fast time, which is extremely difficult. It makes swimming "one of the most complete forms of exercise, but also one of the most demanding." (Traindaly article). The technique needed for swimming is really difficult to master. Swimming "requires near-perfect efficiency to move effectively through the water." (Traindaly article). Unlike most sports, a lack of technique will cause failure to a very large extent. This is a major difficulty, especially considering that there are 4 different strokes, each with their own techniques. As the article notes, it is almost like 4 different sports.

Why Swimmers Stop Swimming Competitively

From the article related to why swimmers leave the sport, it explains many reasons for swimmers quitting, such as pushy parents and coaches, and a lack of social life. Swimmers can recieve a lot of pressure from their coaches and parents. This is probably the main reason swimmers quit. I have gone through this and I know many people who have. It inverts a swimmer's feeling of the sport, causing them to hate it. Parents could push their kid too far. They could make their child go to practices they do not want to go to. Coaches can push swimmers too far similarly to parents, and prevent them from learning from any other source. Parents and coaches are very important towards a swimmer's success, but they can also influence them negatively. Moreover, swimming can limit the rest of a person's life. Swimming is very time consuming. The article says, " committed swimmers often find the biggest draw to the sport is the social circle it comes with." Swimming forces a swimmer to be friends with the people they swim with, or if they have other friends, it makes them have a very limited time socially. It makes it harder to try different hobbies. another reason which is pretty obvious, yet not talked about in the article, is that people like another activity more or just don't like swimming. Ms.Baldwin, my teacher, said, "I like swimming, but there's other things I like more." This is a common reason for not liking a sport.
